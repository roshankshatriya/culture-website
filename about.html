Est. 1999

A Living Heritage of Divine Expression

Sanskriti was founded with a singular vision — to preserve and propagate the sacred art forms of India. Our academy has been a sanctuary for Bharatanatyam, Classical music, and devotional arts for over two decades.

Bharatanatyam, one of the oldest and most celebrated classical dance forms, traces its roots to the Natya Shastra. With intricate footwork, expressive mudras, and soulful abhinaya, it narrates stories of gods and eternal love.

Over the years, we have trained more than 1,200 students, staged over 500 performances nationally and internationally, and upheld the guru-shishya parampara that has kept these traditions alive for millennia.

History

Our Journey Through Time

From humble beginnings to a world-stage presence — every year a new chapter.

1999

Foundation

Sanskriti Academy was founded by Guru Padma Subramaniam with a single classroom and 12 students in Bangalore, dedicated to pure Bharatanatyam training.

2003

First Arangetram

The academy staged its first-ever arangetram — a grand solo debut by student Meera Raghavan at the Chowdiah Memorial Hall, attended by over 1,000 guests.

2007

Carnatic Wing Launched

We expanded our curriculum to include Carnatic vocal and instrumental training under Vidwan Suresh Narayanan, adding a new dimension to the academy's offerings.

2012

International Debut

Sanskriti performed at the Edinburgh International Festival, marking India's classical arts on the global stage and earning widespread critical acclaim.

2018

Vasantha Utsavam Born

Our annual spring cultural festival was inaugurated — bringing together 40+ artists across dance, music, and visual arts for a week-long celebration.

2024

New Campus & Global Reach

A dedicated 10,000 sq.ft. performance and training campus opened its doors. Online programs now connect students in 18+ countries to our guru-lineage training.
